# SOP: Batch Deviation Procedure

## SOP-DEV-001 — Purpose and Scope

This procedure defines the steps for documenting, investigating, and escalating batch deviations at the Chicago Pharma Pilot Plant. All deviations must be reviewed by QA before batch disposition.

## SOP-DEV-002 — Deviation Classification

Classify deviations as minor, major, or critical based on impact to product quality, patient safety, and data integrity. Temperature excursions during fermentation are typically classified as major pending impact assessment.

## SOP-DEV-003 — Immediate Actions

Upon detection of an out-of-spec condition: (1) document the event timestamp, (2) notify shift supervisor, (3) preserve historian data, (4) do not make batch release decisions without QA review.

## SOP-DEV-004 — Investigation Requirements

Investigations must include: affected equipment list, time-series evidence, operator intervention records, related work orders, and calibration status of involved instruments.

## SOP-DEV-005 — Escalation Criteria

Escalate to QA within 4 hours for major deviations. Do not close deviation until root cause analysis is documented and corrective actions are assigned.

## SOP-DEV-006 — Batch Disposition

Only QA may disposition a batch. Manufacturing supervisors may recommend hold or continue processing but cannot authorize release.
